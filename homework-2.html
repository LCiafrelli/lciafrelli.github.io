Homework 2: Distributions and the Caesar Cipher

This assignment explores how probability distributions help in cryptanalysis. A fundamental example is the Caesar Cipher: by looking at the frequency distribution of letters in ciphertext, it's possible to apply statistical inference to uncover the original message. Such analysis is a crucial skill in many classical and modern encryption challenges.

Understanding how distributions operate enables practitioners to detect statistical anomalies in encrypted communications, thereby aiding both codebreaking and the design of more robust ciphers.
