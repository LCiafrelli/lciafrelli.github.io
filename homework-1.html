Homework 1: What is statistics,
and why can it be useful for cybersecurity?

Due: October 4, 2025

Statistics is the science of collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data in order to draw insightful conclusions. In cybersecurity, statistical thinking empowers us to recognize potential threats, build anomaly detection models, and assess risks based on large datasets of events or network behavior. For example, by analyzing login patterns, statistics helps identify brute-force attacks versus normal user activity.

Understanding statistics is essential for designing effective intrusion detection systems, interpreting security alerts, and quantifying uncertainty when making decisions on incident response. It turns raw information into actionable knowledge that strengthens digital defense.
